The Process

How to Brew Beer

Brewing beer follows the same fundamental steps whether you're making a light lager or a rich stout. Each stage builds on the last — master these and you can brew nearly anything.

01

Sanitize Everything

The golden rule of homebrewing. Any contamination will ruin a batch. Use a no-rinse sanitizer like Star San on all equipment before it touches your beer.

02

Mashing the Grain

Steep crushed malted barley in hot water (148–158°F) for 60 minutes. Enzymes convert starches into fermentable sugars, creating your wort.

03

The Boil

Boil the wort for 60–90 minutes. Add hops at different times — early for bitterness, late for aroma. This also sterilizes the wort.

04

Cooling & Pitching

Rapidly cool the wort to fermentation temperature (60–70°F for ales), then add your yeast. Speed matters — slow cooling invites contamination.

05

Fermentation

Yeast consumes the sugars and produces alcohol and CO₂. Most ales ferment in 1–2 weeks. Keep temperatures stable and leave it alone.

06

Bottling & Conditioning

Add a small amount of priming sugar before bottling. Residual yeast carbonates the beer naturally over 2 weeks. Then chill, pour, and enjoy.

What You Need

Equipment & Ingredients

Essential Equipment

  • Large brew kettle (5–8 gallon)
  • Fermentation vessel (carboy or bucket)
  • Airlock & stopper
  • Auto-siphon & tubing
  • Wort chiller (immersion or plate)
  • Hydrometer & thermometer
  • Bottle capper & caps
  • No-rinse sanitizer (Star San)

Key Ingredients

  • Malted barley (base & specialty grains)
  • Hops (pellet or whole cone)
  • Yeast (liquid or dry packets)
  • Water (filtered or adjusted)
  • Priming sugar (corn sugar)
  • Irish moss (fining agent)
  • Yeast nutrient (optional)
  • Fruit / adjuncts (for specialty beers)

Nice to Have

  • Grain mill
  • Mash tun (cooler or kettle)
  • Refractometer
  • pH meter
  • Kegging system (cornelius keg)
  • Temperature controller

Where to Buy

  • Your local homebrew shop (best)
